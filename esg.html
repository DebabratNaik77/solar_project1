ESG is an acronym for Environmental, Social and Governance. It is a set of standards for a company's operations that

  1. ESG stands for Environmental, Social, and Governance factors. It represents a set of criteria used by investors and other stakeholders to evaluate a company's operations and its impact on society and the environment, in addition to traditional financial metrics. Here's a brief breakdown of each component:
  2. Environmental (E): This refers to how a company manages its impact on the natural environment. Factors include its carbon footprint, energy efficiency, waste management, pollution, resource conservation, and efforts to combat climate change.
  3. Social (S): Social factors encompass how a company interacts with its employees, customers, suppliers, communities, and society at large. This includes issues such as labor practices, employee relations, diversity and inclusion, human rights, product safety, community engagement, and philanthropy.
  4. Governance (G): Governance focuses on the systems and structures that oversee the company's operations, including its leadership, board of directors, executive compensation, shareholder rights, transparency, ethics, and adherence to legal and regulatory standards.
